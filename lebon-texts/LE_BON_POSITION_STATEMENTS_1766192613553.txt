**The Crowd: A Study of the Popular Mind (1895)**

1. Individuals in a crowd lose their conscious personality and become subject to unconscious influences.
2. A psychological crowd is a provisional being formed of heterogeneous elements temporarily fused.
3. The crowd mind is characterized by impulsiveness, irritability, and incapacity to reason.
4. Crowds think in images, not in logical sequences.
5. Crowds are credulous and easily influenced by suggestion.
6. The sentiments of crowds are always extreme—either enthusiastic or hostile.
7. Crowds are intolerant and authoritarian by nature.
8. The individual in a crowd descends several rungs on the ladder of civilization.
9. Anonymity in crowds removes the sense of responsibility.
10. Contagion spreads ideas and emotions through crowds with viral rapidity.
11. The prestige of leaders is the primary source of their power over crowds.
12. Affirmation, repetition, and contagion are the principal means of persuading crowds.
13. Crowds cannot distinguish between the subjective and objective.
14. Reason is incapable of combating certain words and formulas in the presence of crowds.
15. The power of words is linked to the images they evoke, not their real meaning.
16. Crowds demand illusions and cannot live without them.
17. The hero worshipped by a crowd is a god to that crowd.
18. Crowds are feminine in character—dominated by unconscious instincts.
19. Education and instruction are incapable of modifying the mental constitution of crowds.
20. Parliamentary assemblies exhibit all the characteristics of crowds.
21. Juries frequently render verdicts that each individual juror would reject.
22. Electoral crowds are influenced more by images and slogans than programs.
23. The age of crowds represents a transitional phase in civilization.
24. Traditions represent the synthesis of a race's accumulated experience.
25. The disappearance of conscious personality is the first step in crowd formation.
