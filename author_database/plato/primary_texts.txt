PLATO - PRIMARY TEXTS INDEX

=========================================
EARLY DIALOGUES (Socratic)
=========================================

**Euthyphro** - What is piety?
**Apology** - Socrates' defense at trial
**Crito** - Should Socrates escape prison?
**Ion** - Poets and inspiration
**Lesser Hippias** - Lying and virtue

=========================================
MIDDLE DIALOGUES (Theory of Forms)
=========================================

**Meno** - Virtue, recollection, learning
**Phaedo** - Soul's immortality, Forms, Socrates' death
**Symposium** - Love, ascent to Beauty
**Republic** - Justice, ideal city, philosopher-kings, Cave allegory
**Phaedrus** - Love, rhetoric, soul as chariot

=========================================
LATE DIALOGUES
=========================================

**Theaetetus** - What is knowledge?
**Parmenides** - Forms critiqued (self-critique)
**Sophist** - Being, non-being
**Statesman** - Political rule
**Philebus** - Pleasure and the good
**Timaeus** - Cosmology, Demiurge creates world
**Critias** - Atlantis story (unfinished)
**Laws** - Second-best city (most realistic)

=========================================
KEY QUOTES
=========================================

**CAVE ALLEGORY** (Republic VII):
"Imagine human beings living in an underground den... here they have been from their childhood, and have their legs and necks chained so that they cannot move, and can only see before them..."

**THE GOOD** (Republic VI):
"The Good is not essence but beyond essence, exceeding it in dignity and power."

**RECOLLECTION** (Meno):
"The soul, being immortal, and having been born many times... has learned everything... So it is no wonder that she should be able to remember virtue and other things."

**PHILOSOPHER-KING** (Republic V):
"Until philosophers rule as kings or those who are now called kings philosophize truly and adequately, there will be no rest from evils for cities."

**FORMS** (Phaedo):
"There is such a thing as the Beautiful itself, and the Good, and the Great... these exist."
