KARL MARX - INDEXED QUOTES

=========================================
CLASS STRUGGLE
=========================================

"The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles."
[Communist Manifesto - Opening thesis]

"Freeman and slave, patrician and plebeian, lord and serf, guild-master and journeyman, in a word, oppressor and oppressed, stood in constant opposition to one another."
[Manifesto - Historical pattern]

"What the bourgeoisie therefore produces, above all, are its own gravediggers. Its fall and the victory of the proletariat are equally inevitable."
[Manifesto - Dialectical reversal]

"Workers of the world, unite! You have nothing to lose but your chains!"
[Manifesto - Revolutionary call]

=========================================
HISTORICAL MATERIALISM
=========================================

"It is not the consciousness of men that determines their being, but, on the contrary, their social being that determines their consciousness."
[Critique of Political Economy, Preface - Core thesis]

"The ideas of the ruling class are in every epoch the ruling ideas."
[German Ideology - Ideology critique]

"Men make their own history, but they do not make it as they please; they do not make it under self-selected circumstances, but under circumstances existing already."
[Eighteenth Brumaire - Historical agency]

"The ruling ideas are nothing more than the ideal expression of the dominant material relationships."
[German Ideology - Base determines superstructure]

=========================================
CAPITALISM & EXPLOITATION
=========================================

"The bourgeoisie, historically, has played a most revolutionary part."
[Manifesto - Bourgeois revolution]

"The bourgeoisie cannot exist without constantly revolutionizing the instruments of production."
[Manifesto - Permanent revolution of production]

"Capital is dead labour, that, vampire-like, only lives by sucking living labour, and lives the more, the more labour it sucks."
[Capital I - Exploitation metaphor]

"The secret of the self-expansion of capital resolves itself into having the disposal of a definite quantity of other people's unpaid labour."
[Capital I - Surplus value]

=========================================
ALIENATION
=========================================

"The worker becomes poorer the more wealth he produces, the more his production increases in power and extent."
[Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts - Alienation]

"The worker becomes an ever cheaper commodity the more commodities he produces."
[EPM - Devaluation of worker]

"The devaluation of the human world grows in direct proportion to the increase in value of the world of things."
[EPM - Inversion]

"Labour produces not only commodities: it produces itself and the worker as a commodity."
[EPM - Self-commodification]

=========================================
RELIGION & IDEOLOGY
=========================================

"Religion is the sigh of the oppressed creature, the heart of a heartless world, and the soul of soulless conditions. It is the opium of the people."
[Critique of Hegel's Philosophy of Right - Famous statement]

"The philosophers have only interpreted the world, in various ways. The point, however, is to change it."
[Theses on Feuerbach, XI - Praxis]

"Social being determines consciousness."
[German Ideology - Materialism]

=========================================
SURPLUS VALUE
=========================================

"Surplus-value is the difference between the value of the product and the value of the elements consumed in the formation of that product."
[Capital I - Definition]

"The rate of surplus-value is an exact expression for the degree of exploitation of labour-power by capital."
[Capital I - Exploitation rate]

"The value of labour-power is the value of the means of subsistence necessary for the maintenance of the labourer."
[Capital I - Wage determination]

=========================================
COMMODITY & VALUE
=========================================

"A commodity is, in the first place, an object outside us, a thing that by its properties satisfies human wants of some sort or another."
[Capital I, Chapter 1 - Opening]

"The value of one commodity is to the value of any other, as the labour time necessary for the production of the one is to that necessary for the production of the other."
[Capital I - Labor theory of value]

"A commodity appears, at first sight, a very trivial thing, and easily understood. Its analysis shows that it is, in reality, a very queer thing, abounding in metaphysical subtleties."
[Capital I - Commodity fetishism introduction]

=========================================
DIALECTICS
=========================================

"Hegel remarks somewhere that all great world-historic facts and personages appear, so to speak, twice. He forgot to add: the first time as tragedy, the second time as farce."
[Eighteenth Brumaire - Dialectical repetition]

"At a certain stage of development, the material productive forces of society come into conflict with the existing relations of production."
[Critique Preface - Revolutionary transformation]

"The contradictions inherent in the movement of capitalist society impress themselves upon the practical bourgeois most strikingly in the changes of the periodic cycle."
[Capital I - Crisis tendency]

=========================================
COMMUNISM
=========================================

"From each according to his ability, to each according to his needs."
[Critique of Gotha Programme - Communist principle]

"In communist society, where nobody has one exclusive sphere of activity... society regulates the general production and thus makes it possible for me to do one thing today and another tomorrow."
[German Ideology - Division of labor overcome]

"The free development of each is the condition for the free development of all."
[Manifesto - Communist ideal]

=========================================
STATE & REVOLUTION
=========================================

"The executive of the modern state is but a committee for managing the common affairs of the whole bourgeoisie."
[Manifesto - State as class instrument]

"Between capitalist and communist society there lies the period of the revolutionary transformation of the one into the other. Corresponding to this is also a political transition period in which the state can be nothing but the revolutionary dictatorship of the proletariat."
[Critique of Gotha - Transition period]

"Let the ruling classes tremble at a communist revolution."
[Manifesto - Revolutionary threat]

=========================================
CAPITALISM'S CONTRADICTIONS
=========================================

"Accumulation of wealth at one pole is, therefore, at the same time accumulation of misery at the opposite pole."
[Capital I - Polarization]

"The means of production and of exchange, on whose foundation the bourgeoisie built itself up, were generated in feudal society."
[Manifesto - Historical dialectic]

"Modern bourgeois society, with its relations of production, of exchange and of property, a society that has conjured up such gigantic means of production and of exchange, is like the sorcerer who is no longer able to control the powers of the nether world whom he has called up by his spells."
[Manifesto - Loss of control]

=========================================
PRODUCTION & LABOR
=========================================

"Labour is, in the first place, a process in which both man and Nature participate, and in which man of his own accord starts, regulates, and controls the material re-actions between himself and Nature."
[Capital I - Labor process]

"The less you eat, drink, and read books; the less you go to the theatre, the dance hall, the public house; the less you think, love, theorize, sing, paint, fence, etc., the more you save—the greater becomes your treasure which neither moths nor dust will devour—your capital."
[EPM - Asceticism of capital]

=========================================
BOURGEOISIE & PROLETARIAT
=========================================

"The bourgeoisie has stripped of its halo every occupation hitherto honoured and looked up to with reverent awe. It has converted the physician, the lawyer, the priest, the poet, the man of science, into its paid wage labourers."
[Manifesto - Commodification]

"The proletariat goes through various stages of development. With its birth begins its struggle with the bourgeoisie."
[Manifesto - Class formation]

"The advance of industry, whose involuntary promoter is the bourgeoisie, replaces the isolation of the labourers, due to competition, by their revolutionary combination, due to association."
[Manifesto - Organization]

=========================================
INTERNATIONAL REVOLUTION
=========================================

"The Communists are further reproached with desiring to abolish countries and nationality. The working men have no country."
[Manifesto - Internationalism]

"National differences and antagonisms between peoples are daily more and more vanishing, owing to the development of the bourgeoisie, to freedom of commerce, to the world market."
[Manifesto - Globalization]
