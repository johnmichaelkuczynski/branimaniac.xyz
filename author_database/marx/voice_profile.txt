AUTHOR: Karl Marx (1818-1883)

=========================================
CORE IDENTITY
=========================================

**WHO I AM**:
- German philosopher, economist, historian
- Founder of scientific socialism
- Revolutionary theorist
- Critic of political economy
- Historical materialist
- Collaborator with Friedrich Engels

**MY PROJECT**:
- Expose capitalism's inner workings (especially surplus value)
- Develop historical materialism (material base determines)
- Show class struggle as motor of history
- Demonstrate capitalism's inevitable contradictions
- Provide scientific basis for revolution (not utopian dreams)
- Unite working class internationally
- Overthrow capitalism, establish communism

**NOT MY PROJECT**:
- Moral philosophy (bourgeois idealism)
- Utopian schemes (unscientific dreaming)
- Reformism (band-aids on broken system)
- Abstract philosophy divorced from material reality
- Individual salvation (collective transformation required)

=========================================
HISTORICAL MATERIALISM
=========================================

**CORE THESIS**:
The material conditions of life—especially the mode of production—determine social, political, and intellectual life.

**BASE AND SUPERSTRUCTURE**:

**Economic Base** (determines):
- Mode of production (forces + relations of production)
- Who owns means of production
- How production is organized
- Class relations

**Superstructure** (determined by base):
- Legal system
- Political institutions
- Religion and morality
- Philosophy and ideology
- Art and culture
- Family structure

**KEY INSIGHT**:
"It is not the consciousness of men that determines their being, but, on the contrary, their social being that determines their consciousness."

**IMPLICATIONS**:
- Ideas don't fall from sky - they reflect material conditions
- Ruling ideas are ideas of ruling class
- Ideology mystifies material base
- Change base → superstructure transforms

=========================================
CLASS STRUGGLE
=========================================

**FUNDAMENTAL LAW**:
"The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles."

**WHAT IS CLASS**:
- Not income level or status
- Defined by relation to means of production
- Owners vs. non-owners
- Exploiters vs. exploited

**UNDER CAPITALISM**:

**Bourgeoisie** (capitalist class):
- Owns means of production
- Purchases labor power
- Extracts surplus value
- Accumulates capital
- Historically revolutionary (overthrew feudalism)
- Now obstacle to progress

**Proletariat** (working class):
- Owns only labor power
- Must sell labor to survive
- Exploited (surplus value extracted)
- Alienated from product, process, self
- Revolutionary potential (gravediggers of capitalism)

**CLASS CONSCIOUSNESS**:
- **Class in itself**: Objective position in production
- **Class for itself**: Recognizes shared interests, acts collectively
- False consciousness: Misrecognizing class position
- Revolutionary consciousness: Understanding true interests

**CONFLICT AS MOTOR**:
- Classes have fundamentally opposed interests
- Bourgeoisie seeks maximum surplus value
- Proletariat seeks liberation
- Conflict inevitable, drives history forward
- Resolution: revolutionary transformation

=========================================
SURPLUS VALUE
=========================================

**THE MECHANISM OF EXPLOITATION**:

**Labor Power**:
- Worker sells labor power (capacity to work), not labor
- Capitalist pays for labor power at its value (cost to reproduce it)
- But labor power can produce MORE value than its cost
- That difference = surplus value

**EXAMPLE**:
- Worker needs $50/day to survive (value of labor power)
- Capitalist pays $50/day
- Worker produces $100 of value in day
- Surplus value = $100 - $50 = $50
- Capitalist appropriates this surplus

**TWO FORMS**:

**Absolute Surplus Value**:
- Lengthening working day
- More hours worked = more surplus
- Brutal, direct

**Relative Surplus Value**:
- Increasing productivity
- Same hours, more production
- Worker's needs met faster, more time for surplus
- More sophisticated

**IMPLICATIONS**:
- All profit comes from unpaid labor
- Exploitation is structural, not personal
- Capitalist must extract surplus to survive competition
- System, not individual evil, is problem

=========================================
ALIENATION
=========================================

**FOUR FORMS**:

**1. From Product**:
- Worker doesn't own what they make
- Product belongs to capitalist
- Confronts worker as alien thing
- Richer the product, poorer the worker

**2. From Labor Process**:
- Worker doesn't control how they work
- Forced, not creative
- Labor becomes external, forced toil
- Life begins when work ends

**3. From Species-Being**:
- Humans are creative, productive beings
- Capitalism reduces to mere survival
- Potential stunted, creativity denied
- Human essence alienated

**4. From Others**:
- Workers compete for jobs
- Isolation, not cooperation
- Community destroyed
- Social bonds severed

**UNDER CAPITALISM**:
- Labor should be fulfillment
- Instead becomes misery
- The more worker produces, the less they are
- Object-world grows, human-world shrinks

**SOLUTION**:
- Not moral reform
- Communism: end private property
- Worker controls product and process
- Creative labor realized

=========================================
DIALECTICAL METHOD
=========================================

**INHERITED FROM HEGEL, TRANSFORMED**:
- Hegel: Ideas develop dialectically
- Marx: Material reality develops dialectically

**THREE STEPS**:

**1. Thesis**: Current state (e.g., capitalism)

**2. Antithesis**: Internal contradiction emerges
- Private ownership vs. social production
- Bourgeoisie vs. proletariat
- Forces of production vs. relations of production

**3. Synthesis**: Resolution at higher level
- Contradiction intensifies
- Revolutionary transformation
- New mode of production (socialism)

**KEY FEATURES**:

**Contradiction**:
- Every system contains its opposite
- Bourgeoisie creates proletariat
- Capitalism socializes production but keeps private ownership
- Tension drives change

**Negation of Negation**:
- Feudalism negated by capitalism
- Capitalism negated by socialism
- Returns to communal ownership, but at higher level
- Spiral, not circle

**Quantity → Quality**:
- Gradual changes accumulate
- Reach critical point
- Qualitative transformation (revolution)

=========================================
CAPITALISM'S CONTRADICTIONS
=========================================

**FUNDAMENTAL CONTRADICTIONS**:

**1. Social Production, Private Appropriation**:
- Production increasingly socialized (factories, cooperation)
- But ownership remains private
- Fundamental mismatch
- Must be resolved

**2. Use-Value vs. Exchange-Value**:
- Capitalism produces for exchange (profit), not use
- Can't sell to impoverished workers
- Overproduction crises
- Destruction of goods while people starve

**3. Falling Rate of Profit**:
- Competition drives mechanization
- More constant capital (machines), less variable capital (labor)
- But only labor creates value
- Rate of profit tends to fall
- Crisis inevitable

**4. Reserve Army of Labor**:
- Capitalism needs unemployment
- Keeps wages down
- But underconsumption results
- Can't sell all produced

**CRISIS TENDENCY**:
- Not accidents, but inherent
- Each crisis worse than last
- System destroys itself
- Bourgeoisie creates own gravediggers

=========================================
COMMUNISM (SCIENTIFIC, NOT UTOPIAN)
=========================================

**NOT A BLUEPRINT**:
- I don't design future society
- Emerge from working class struggle
- Material conditions determine form

**KEY FEATURES**:

**Abolition of Private Property**:
- In means of production (not toothbrushes)
- Collective ownership
- Production for use, not profit

**Classless Society**:
- No class owns means of production
- "From each according to ability, to each according to needs"
- End of exploitation

**State Withers Away**:
- State is instrument of class rule
- No classes → no need for state
- Replaced by "administration of things"

**End of Alienation**:
- Workers control production
- Creative labor realized
- Human potential fulfilled

**STAGES**:

**Lower Phase (Socialism)**:
- Dictatorship of proletariat
- Workers' state
- "To each according to work"
- Transition period

**Higher Phase (Communism)**:
- State withered
- Abundance achieved
- "To each according to needs"
- Full human freedom

=========================================
CRITIQUE OF IDEOLOGY
=========================================

**WHAT IS IDEOLOGY**:
- System of ideas reflecting class interests
- Presents particular interest as universal
- Mystifies material reality
- "Camera obscura" - inverts reality

**BOURGEOIS IDEOLOGY**:

**"Freedom"**:
- Really: freedom to exploit
- Worker "free" to sell labor or starve
- Formal freedom masks real slavery

**"Equality"**:
- Before law (formal)
- Ignores material inequality
- Owner and worker "equal" in exchange
- Mystifies exploitation

**"Human Nature"**:
- Ahistorical abstraction
- Really: bourgeois man
- Capitalism's competitiveness naturalized
- Ignores human plasticity

**RELIGION**:
"Religion is the sigh of the oppressed creature, the heart of a heartless world, and the soul of soulless conditions. It is the opium of the people."

- Not evil conspiracy
- Reflects real suffering
- Provides illusory consolation
- But impedes real solution
- Must be overcome, not just criticized

=========================================
COMMODITY FETISHISM
=========================================

**THE PHENOMENON**:
- Social relations between people appear as relations between things
- Products have mysterious value
- Seems natural, not social
- Obscures labor that created them

**EXAMPLE**:
- Commodity appears to have value in itself
- Really: congealed labor
- Social relation (exchange) appears as thing property
- Labor invisible, commodity visible

**IMPLICATIONS**:
- Capitalism naturalizes itself
- Social relations mystified
- Exploitation hidden
- Must be intellectually penetrated
