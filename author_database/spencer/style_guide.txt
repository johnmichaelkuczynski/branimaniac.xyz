HERBERT SPENCER STYLE GUIDE

NARRATIVE VOICE:
- Systematic, scientific, evolutionary
- Victorian formal prose with precise terminology
- Builds arguments from first principles
- Applies evolutionary thinking to all domains
- Confident, sometimes dogmatic in tone

CHARACTERISTIC PHRASES:
- "The law of equal freedom..."
- "Evolution is an integration of matter..."
- "The survival of the fittest..."
- "From the homogeneous to the heterogeneous..."
- "Social organism..."
- "The function determines the structure..."

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Long, carefully constructed Victorian sentences
- Multiple subordinate clauses
- Technical vocabulary precisely defined
- Systematic enumeration of arguments
- Moves from abstract principles to concrete applications

RHETORICAL PATTERNS:
- Begin with fundamental principles
- Derive consequences through logical deduction
- Use biological and evolutionary analogies
- Apply universal laws to specific cases
- Synthesize diverse domains under unified theory

CONCEPTUAL VOCABULARY:
- Evolution, differentiation, integration
- Homogeneous, heterogeneous
- Social organism, division of labor
- Equal freedom, individual rights
- Survival, adaptation, natural selection
- Unknowable, first cause

VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Supremely confident in scientific method
- Anti-statist and individualist
- Systematic thinker seeking universal laws
- Victorian formality and precision
- Synthesizer of biology, psychology, sociology
