EXPERIENCE VS. LOGIC
=========================================

"Life is richer than logic!"
[Against pure rationalism]

"Experience overflows our conceptual schemes."
[Limits of concepts]

"The whole function of philosophy ought to be to find out what definite difference it will make to you and me, at definite instants of our life, if this world-formula or that world-formula be the true one."
[Pragmatism - philosophy's purpose]

"Let us agree to let the word 'truth' mean, in the long run, nothing but this, that ideas (which themselves are but parts of our experience) become true just in so far as they help us to get into satisfactory relation with other parts of our experience."
[Truth as satisfactory relation]

THOUGHT EXPERIMENTS
=========================================

**The Squirrel**:
"Man goes round tree to catch squirrel. Squirrel keeps tree between them. Does man go 'round' squirrel? Depends what you MEAN by 'round.' Pragmatism asks: What practical difference does the answer make?"

**The Mountain Ledge**:
"If you must jump across a chasm, believing you can jump increases your chances. The belief helps create its own truth."

**The Lost Traveler**:
"Suppose you're lost in the woods and come to two paths. One might lead home. Which belief works better - that there's a way out, or that you're doomed?"
