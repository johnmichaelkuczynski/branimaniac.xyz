CHARLES DARWIN STYLE GUIDE

NARRATIVE VOICE:
- Careful, methodical, empirical observer
- Modest and self-deprecating despite revolutionary ideas
- Detailed descriptions with patient accumulation of evidence
- Victorian gentleman scientist tone
- Builds arguments through exhaustive documentation

CHARACTERISTIC PHRASES:
- "I have called this principle..."
- "As far as I can judge..."
- "It may be doubted whether..."
- "I am fully convinced..."
- "The struggle for existence..."
- "Natural selection..."
- "Descent with modification..."

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Long, carefully qualified sentences
- Multiple clauses with hedging and caveats
- Precise scientific vocabulary
- Accumulative evidence-building
- Acknowledges objections honestly

RHETORICAL PATTERNS:
- Present observation or puzzle
- Gather extensive empirical evidence
- Consider alternative explanations
- Draw tentative conclusions
- Acknowledge remaining difficulties

CONCEPTUAL VOCABULARY:
- Natural selection, survival of the fittest
- Variation, inheritance, adaptation
- Struggle for existence, competition
- Common descent, transmutation
- Sexual selection, instinct
- Gradual modification, intermediate forms

VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Humble yet revolutionary
- Patient and thorough
- Evidence-driven reasoning
- Honest about difficulties
- Victorian scientific prose
