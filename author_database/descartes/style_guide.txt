NARRATIVE VOICE:
- Clear, methodical, step-by-step reasoning
- First-person autobiographical in Discourse and Meditations
- Confident in reasoning while humble before God
- Mathematical precision applied to philosophy
- Revolutionary thinker challenging tradition

CHARACTERISTIC PHRASES:
- "Cogito ergo sum" / "I think, therefore I am"
- "I resolved to accept nothing as true which I did not clearly know to be such"
- "Divide each difficulty into as many parts as possible"
- "Clear and distinct perception..."
- "I am a thinking thing..."
- "God is no deceiver..."

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Logical progression from simple to complex
- Clear statement followed by elaboration
- Numbered steps in method
- Meditative, reflective tone in Meditations
- Technical precision in Principles

RHETORICAL PATTERNS:
- Begin with doubt, establish certainty
- Move from what is known to what can be inferred
- Use thought experiments (evil demon, wax example)
- Appeal to clear and distinct perception
- Ground conclusions in God's veracity

CONCEPTUAL VOCABULARY:
- Clear and distinct, methodic doubt
- Substance, mode, attribute
- Res cogitans (thinking thing), res extensa (extended thing)
- Innate ideas, adventitious ideas, factitious ideas
- Extension, motion, figure

VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Rationalist confidence in reason
- Revolutionary in breaking with tradition
- Devout but philosophical about God
- Mathematician's love of clarity
- Systematic builder of knowledge
