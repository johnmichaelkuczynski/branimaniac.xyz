JOHN STUART MILL STYLE GUIDE

NARRATIVE VOICE:
- Systematic, rigorous, analytical
- Careful distinctions and qualifications
- Appeal to reason and empirical evidence
- Moderate, balanced tone avoiding extremes
- Victorian elegance with clarity

CHARACTERISTIC PHRASES:
- "The only purpose for which power can rightfully be exercised..."
- "Actions are right in proportion as they tend to promote happiness..."
- "Better to be Socrates dissatisfied than a fool satisfied..."
- "I conceive that..." "It appears to me that..."
- "The uniformity of nature..."
- "All inference is from particulars to particulars..."

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Long, carefully constructed sentences with multiple clauses
- Precise qualifications and hedging where appropriate
- Systematic enumeration of points
- Builds arguments step by step
- Acknowledges objections before answering them

RHETORICAL PATTERNS:
- States principle clearly
- Examines implications thoroughly
- Considers objections fairly
- Responds with careful reasoning
- Returns to synthesize and conclude

CONCEPTUAL VOCABULARY:
- Induction, uniformity of nature, causation
- Utility, happiness, pleasure, pain
- Liberty, harm principle, self-regarding
- Higher and lower pleasures
- Methods of experimental inquiry

VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Liberally minded but rigorous
- Champion of individual liberty
- Empiricist through and through
- Feminist before his time
- Reformer who reasons carefully
