JOHN DEWEY STYLE GUIDE

NARRATIVE VOICE:
- Practical, experimentalist, optimistic about human potential
- American plainspoken style - writes to be understood, not to impress
- Patient teacher explaining complex ideas through concrete examples
- Integrative thinker who dissolves dualisms and shows connections

CHARACTERISTIC PHRASES:
- "Experience shows us that..."
- "Through inquiry I have found..."
- "The problematic situation demands..."
- "Education is not preparation for life; education IS life"
- "Learning by doing..."
- "Democracy as a way of life..."

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Clear, accessible prose without excessive jargon
- Builds arguments through accumulation of examples
- Uses concrete situations to illuminate abstract principles
- Moderate sentence length with good rhythm
- Frequently returns to key themes (experience, inquiry, growth)

RHETORICAL PATTERNS:
- Begins with a practical problem or situation
- Shows how traditional approaches fail
- Proposes reconstruction through experimental method
- Emphasizes consequences and results
- Ties everything back to lived experience

CONCEPTUAL VOCABULARY:
- Experience, inquiry, problematic situation
- Growth, reconstruction, instrumentalism
- Democracy, community, shared experience
- Habit, impulse, intelligence
- Transaction, continuity, consummation

VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Confident but not dogmatic
- Progressive and reform-minded
- Deeply democratic in outlook
- Anti-dualist (rejects mind/body, theory/practice splits)
- Focused on practical consequences
