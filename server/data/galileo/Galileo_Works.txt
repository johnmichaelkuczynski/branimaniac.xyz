GALILEO POSITION STATEMENTS

## SIDEREUS NUNCIUS (1610)

1. The moon's surface is rough, uneven, and full of cavities and prominences—not a perfect sphere
2. Lunar mountains exist and can be measured geometrically by their shadows
3. The boundary between lunar light and dark regions is irregular, not a smooth arc
4. The moon has no intrinsic light but reflects sunlight
5. Earthshine on the moon is reflected light from Earth
6. Earth itself would appear luminous when viewed from the moon
7. The Milky Way is composed of innumerable individual stars
8. Nebulae resolve into clusters of discrete stars under magnification
9. The fixed stars appear as points rather than discs through the telescope
10. Jupiter has four satellites (the Medicean stars) orbiting it
11. Jupiter's moons complete their orbits in different periods
12. The existence of Jovian moons refutes the objection that Earth cannot move while retaining its moon
13. The telescope reveals far more stars than are visible to the naked eye
14. Star magnitudes as traditionally classified are unreliable
15. The telescope is a reliable instrument for astronomical observation
16. Mathematical reasoning can determine physical properties of celestial bodies
17. The heavens are not fundamentally different in kind from Earth
18. Observational evidence should override traditional astronomical authority
19. The Ptolemaic distinction between celestial and terrestrial realms is false
20. New discoveries require revision of inherited cosmological frameworks

---

## LETTERS ON SUNSPOTS (1613)

1. Sunspots are on or very near the sun's surface, not distant planets
2. The sun rotates on its axis approximately monthly
3. Sunspots are generated and dissolve—they are not permanent bodies
4. Sunspots resemble terrestrial clouds or smoke in their behavior
5. The sun is not an immutable, perfect celestial body
6. Aristotelian celestial immutability is empirically false
7. Venus exhibits a full range of phases like the moon
8. The phases of Venus prove it orbits the sun, not Earth
9. The Copernican system explains the phases of Venus; the Ptolemaic cannot
10. Mercury also exhibits phases consistent with solar orbit
11. Observation trumps philosophical speculation about celestial matters
12. The heavens undergo generation and corruption like terrestrial nature
13. Traditional physics cannot account for actual celestial phenomena
14. Priority disputes (with Scheiner) must be settled by documented evidence
15. The telescope provides access to truths unavailable to naked-eye astronomy
16. Solar phenomena can be studied systematically through repeated observation
17. Mathematical analysis applies to solar as well as planetary phenomena
18. The distinction between sublunary and superlunary physics is untenable
19. Copernicanism is not merely a computational device but physically true
20. Natural philosophy must be reformed on the basis of new observations

---

## IL SAGGIATORE (1623)

1. Philosophy is written in the language of mathematics
2. Nature's characters are triangles, circles, and geometrical figures
3. Without mathematics, natural philosophy is incomprehensible
4. Qualities like taste, odor, and color exist only in the perceiving subject
5. Primary qualities (shape, size, motion) are objective; secondary qualities are subjective
6. Heat is produced by motion of minute particles
7. Sensory qualities are caused by motions and configurations of matter
8. The distinction between real and perceived properties is fundamental
9. Comets are likely sublunary optical phenomena, not solid bodies
10. Arguments from authority carry no weight in natural philosophy
11. Experimental evidence must guide natural investigation
12. Poetic language is inappropriate for scientific discourse
13. Precision in language is essential to avoiding philosophical error
14. Natural phenomena require mechanical explanation
15. Traditional natural philosophy is verbose and empty of content
16. A single demonstrative proof outweighs a thousand probable arguments
17. Scientific disputes must be resolved by evidence and reasoning, not status
18. Nature does not act through occult qualities
19. Atomistic or corpuscular matter theory is preferable to hylomorphism
20. Science proceeds by analysis into simple components and mathematical relations

---

## DIALOGUE CONCERNING THE TWO CHIEF WORLD SYSTEMS (1632)

1. Earth rotates on its axis daily
2. Earth revolves around the sun annually
3. The apparent motion of the heavens is caused by Earth's real motion
4. Stellar parallax is undetectable because of the stars' immense distance
5. Falling bodies continue to share Earth's motion—there is no detectable effect of rotation
6. Terrestrial physics operates identically whether Earth moves or rests
7. The tides are caused by the combined effects of Earth's rotation and revolution
8. The Copernican system is simpler and more coherent than the Ptolemaic
9. Retrograde planetary motion is naturally explained by Earth's orbital motion
10. The Ptolemaic system requires arbitrary and unconnected epicycles
11. Uniform circular motion is not the only natural celestial motion
12. The moon always shows the same face because of synchronized rotation
13. Arguments from common sense against Earth's motion are fallacious
14. Relative motion cannot be detected by observers within a moving system
15. Ship's-hold thought experiments demonstrate relativity of motion
16. Aristotelian physics contradicts observational evidence
17. Scriptural interpretation must accommodate demonstrated physical truths
18. Natural philosophy cannot be conducted by syllogistic logic alone
19. The fixed stars are suns at varying immense distances
20. The Copernican system has physical reality, not merely predictive utility

## DISCOURSES ON TWO NEW SCIENCES (1638)

1. The strength of materials does not scale proportionally with size
2. Larger structures require disproportionately more material for equal strength
3. There are natural limits to the size of animals and structures
4. Beams break in predictable ways depending on their dimensions and loading
5. Resistance to fracture can be calculated mathematically
6. Freely falling bodies accelerate uniformly regardless of weight
7. Distance fallen is proportional to the square of elapsed time
8. Velocity of falling bodies increases linearly with time
9. Projectile motion is composed of uniform horizontal and accelerated vertical motion
10. The trajectory of a projectile is a parabola
11. The speed at the end of descent is the same regardless of the incline's angle
12. Pendulum period is independent of amplitude for small swings
13. Pendulum period depends on length, not on the weight of the bob
14. Naturally accelerated motion follows precise mathematical laws
15. The mean-speed theorem correctly relates distance to uniformly accelerated motion
16. Air resistance affects falling bodies but can be abstracted from in analysis
17. Percussion force can be analyzed as accumulated momentum
18. Vibrating strings produce frequencies proportional to tension and inversely to length
19. Consonance is explained by the ratios of vibrational frequencies
20. Infinite quantities can be meaningfully compared through one-to-one correspondence

## LETTER TO THE GRAND DUCHESS CHRISTINA (1615)

1. Scripture and nature both derive from God and cannot truly conflict
2. Scripture accommodates its language to the understanding of common people
3. Passages describing natural phenomena should not be interpreted literally when science demonstrates otherwise
4. The purpose of Scripture is salvation, not natural philosophy
5. "The Bible teaches how to go to heaven, not how the heavens go" (via Baronius)
6. Scientific demonstrations should constrain biblical interpretation
7. Augustine supported non-literal interpretation when science demands it
8. The Church Fathers did not intend to fix cosmological doctrines
9. The Joshua passage can be better interpreted under Copernicanism than Ptolemaism
10. Stopping the sun in Ptolemaic physics would not extend the day as described
11. Theological authorities should not prematurely condemn scientific positions
12. Condemning Copernicanism would harm the Church if later proven true
13. Heresy concerns doctrine necessary for salvation, not physical astronomy
14. Natural philosophers have competence in their domain that theologians should respect
15. Prohibiting free inquiry damages the pursuit of truth
16. Scientific matters should be decided by evidence and demonstration
17. Ecclesiastical censure of science risks making the Church appear ignorant
18. The Copernican hypothesis has strong empirical support
19. Prudence requires allowing scientific questions to remain open
20. Faith and reason operate in complementary, not competing, domains
