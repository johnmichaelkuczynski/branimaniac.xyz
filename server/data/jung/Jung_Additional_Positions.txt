### Psychology and Alchemy (CW 12)
* The alchemical process begins with the prima materia, which symbolizes the raw, undifferentiated state of the psyche before individuation.
* The nigredo stage represents the initial confrontation with the shadow, leading to psychological dissolution and despair.
* The albedo phase signifies purification and the emergence of clarity, often linked to the integration of the anima.
* The rubedo is the final reddening, symbolizing the achievement of wholeness and the philosopher's stone as the self.
* Alchemical symbols like the hermaphrodite illustrate the reconciliation of masculine and feminine opposites within the individual.
* The pelican feeding its young with its own blood is a motif for self-sacrifice necessary in psychic transformation.
* The aurum non vulgi (non-vulgar gold) refers to spiritual enlightenment rather than material wealth.
* The vas hermeticum (hermetic vessel) acts as a container for the alchemical work, paralleling the analytic relationship.
* The multiplicatio stage involves the amplification of the achieved insight, spreading it throughout the personality.
* Alchemical texts like the Rosarium Philosophorum depict the stages of the coniunctio as a model for psychic integration.
