SPINOZA COMPLETE POSITION STATEMENTS

=== ETHICS (1677) ===

1. God is the single substance.
2. Substance is infinite.
3. Substance is self-caused.
4. God and Nature are identical.
5. Nothing exists outside God.
6. Modes are the affections of substance.
7. Thought and Extension are parallel attributes.
8. The order of ideas is the same as the order of things.
9. Mind and body are one and the same thing expressed in two ways.
10. The human mind is the idea of the human body.
11. The mind perceives only the body and its modifications.
12. Will and intellect are identical.
13. There is no free will.
14. Everything is determined by necessity.
15. Adequate ideas express true causes.
16. Inadequate ideas express partial or confused causes.
17. Error is privation of knowledge.
18. Imagination is the first kind of knowledge.
19. Reason is the second kind of knowledge.
20. Intuition is the third kind of knowledge.
21. The more adequate our ideas, the more we share in God's nature.
22. The essence of man is conatus.
23. Conatus is the striving to persevere in being.
24. Virtue is power.
25. Virtue is identical with acting from adequate ideas.
26. Joy is increase in one's power of acting.
27. Sadness is decrease in one's power of acting.
28. Love is joy accompanied by an external cause.
29. Hatred is sadness accompanied by an external cause.
30. Hope is inconstant joy arising from doubt.
31. Fear is inconstant sadness arising from doubt.
32. Desire is the essence of man insofar as it is conceived as determined to act.
33. Good is what we certainly know is useful to us.
34. Evil is what we certainly know prevents the attainment of good.
35. There are no absolute moral values.
36. The mind suffers passive emotions when it has inadequate ideas.
37. Active emotions arise from adequate ideas.
38. Freedom is acting from one's own nature.
39. Bondage is being governed by passions.
40. The freer a person, the less they hate.
41. The freer a person, the less they fear death.
42. The mind's highest good is knowledge of God.
43. The mind's highest virtue is knowing God.
44. Intellectual love of God is eternal.
45. The mind is eternal insofar as it possesses adequate ideas.
46. The mind's eternity is not personal immortality.
47. All finite things are determinate modes of God's attributes.
48. God's power is infinite.
49. God's essence involves necessary existence.
50. God acts solely from the necessity of his nature.
51. God is not a final cause.
52. God does not act for ends.
53. Teleology is a projection of human imagination.
54. Nature has no moral order.
55. Human beings are part of nature, not a kingdom within a kingdom.
56. Human actions follow natural laws like everything else.
57. Freedom is understanding necessity.
58. Love toward God is the highest blessedness.
59. The more we understand things, the less we are subject to passions.
60. Knowledge of the causes of emotions transforms them.
61. Hate can be destroyed by love.
62. Pity is not a virtue.
63. Repentance is not a virtue.
64. Humility is not a virtue.
65. Self-esteem is a virtue.
66. Pride is a passion.
67. Shame is a passion.
68. Envy arises from sadness at another's good.
69. Compassion arises from imagining another's misfortune.
70. People agree in nature when they live according to reason.
71. People are naturally antagonistic when dominated by passions.
72. Reason seeks peace.
73. Reason seeks cooperation.
74. The state is justified by the power of the multitude.
75. Natural right equals natural power.
76. In the state of nature, everyone has a right to everything they can do.
77. Civil order arises when individuals transfer power to a sovereign.
78. The sovereign's right is the collective power of the citizens.
79. Obedience is required when laws serve the common good.
80. Freedom of thought cannot be surrendered.
81. A free state allows freedom of philosophical inquiry.
82. The best state encourages rational life.
83. Democracy is the most natural form of state.
84. The body's complexity surpasses all human understanding.
85. The mind is the idea of that complex body.
86. Emotions vary with bodily constitution.
87. No two people share the same nature exactly.
88. Love increases when accompanied by idea of mutual love.
89. Hate increases when we imagine the hated person hates us back.
90. We strive to make others love what we love.
91. We strive to make others hate what we hate.
92. We feel joy when we imagine others like us.
93. We feel sadness when we imagine others unlike us.
94. Contempt is imagination of another's powerlessness.
95. Wonder arises from novelty of an idea.
96. Devotion is love toward one we admire.
97. Gratitude is love toward one who has benefited us.
98. Revenge is desire arising from hatred.
99. Nobility is desire to help others from rational understanding.
100. Blessedness is the intellectual love of God.

=== TRACTATUS THEOLOGICO-POLITICUS (1670) ===

1. Scripture teaches obedience, not metaphysics.
2. Theology and philosophy must be kept separate.
3. True religion is justice and charity.
4. Prophecy is imagination, not supernatural knowledge.
5. Prophets grasp moral truths, not abstract truths.
6. Miracles are natural events misunderstood.
7. Nature never violates its own laws.
8. The Bible uses the language of the common people.
9. Scripture contains errors of fact.
10. Scripture must be interpreted historically.
11. The meaning of Scripture equals the meaning intended by its human authors.
12. No doctrinal proposition can be proved from ambiguous texts.
13. Faith requires obedience, not knowledge.
14. Faith and reason have different domains.
15. Philosophers must be free to interpret nature.
16. The state must control religious authority.
17. Religious leaders must not have civil power.
18. Political stability requires freedom of thought.
19. The goal of the state is security and freedom.
20. The sovereign alone decides public religious practice.
21. Private belief cannot be compelled.
22. Civil law overrides ecclesiastical law.
23. Sedition arises when clergy claim independent authority.
24. Superstition thrives where people fear and are ignorant.
25. The more divided the religious institutions, the weaker the state.
26. Hebrew commonwealth was a theocracy.
27. The Mosaic law was tailored for one nation and one era.
28. The Jews were chosen only in a political sense.
29. Biblical laws are not eternally binding on all peoples.
30. True piety does not depend on ceremonies.
31. Ceremonial laws preserve obedience, not truth.
32. Mosaic ceremonies were political institutions.
33. After the destruction of the Hebrew state, Mosaic law lost its force.
34. Christ taught universal moral truth, not ceremonial law.
35. Apostolic writings adapted doctrine to their audiences.
36. Scripture's authority is practical, not philosophical.
37. Canon formation was historical, not miraculous.
38. Biblical books were compiled long after the events.
39. Many biblical texts are edited composites.
40. Authors like Moses did not write every text attributed to them.
41. The Pentateuch shows multiple hands and anachronisms.
42. Biblical contradiction is compatible with divine purpose.
43. Divine law aims at moral transformation.
44. Philosophical truth must come from reason, not Scripture.
45. Philosophy cannot be censored without harming the state.
46. The state must permit free speech unless it incites rebellion.
47. Free philosophical inquiry strengthens religion.
48. Obedience to civil law is the essence of civic virtue.
49. People obey better when allowed to think freely.
50. Democratic states approach natural equality most closely.
51. Monarchies and aristocracies easily misuse religion.
52. Tyrants encourage superstition to control people.
53. The safest republics separate clerical and civil power.
54. True freedom is freedom from fear.
55. Divine providence is identical with natural law.
56. Miracles are impossible because nature is uniform.
57. Revelation accommodates itself to human imagination.
58. Biblical style is figurative, not literal.
59. Scripture must be understood by studying its language.
60. Scripture must be read with knowledge of its history.
61. Scripture must be interpreted through its internal coherence.
62. The Bible is sufficient for salvation but not for philosophy.
63. States decline when religious zeal overrides civil authority.
64. Peace depends on rational governance.
65. Religion is useful when subordinated to the state.
66. The best government fosters both piety and freedom.
67. No one can transfer the freedom to think, even if they try.
68. The sovereign cannot command belief, only action.
69. Faith becomes superstition when it demands ignorance.
70. True religion unites citizens; superstition divides them.

=== TRACTATUS POLITICUS (1677) ===

1. Natural right equals natural power.
2. In nature, each being has a right to everything it can do.
3. Civil right arises when individuals unite their powers.
4. The state's right equals the collective power of the people.
5. The goal of the state is peace and security.
6. The best state maximizes individual freedom.
7. Freedom equals living under laws one helps establish.
8. Reason seeks peace and cooperation.
9. The sovereign's power is limited by what subjects will actually obey.
10. Absolute sovereignty exists only in theory.
11. Citizens never fully surrender the power of judgment.
12. No state can prevent inward dissent.
13. Obedience is maintained by common advantage.
14. Laws must align with human nature.
15. People obey better when laws support their natural desires.
16. Fear-based rule is unstable.
17. Hope-based rule is more durable.
18. Sedition arises from public fear or indignation.
19. The sovereign must avoid humiliating subjects.
20. A well-ordered state prevents factionalism.
21. The power of the state depends on the unity of the people.
22. Democracy best preserves natural equality.
23. Aristocracy is superior to monarchy in stability.
24. Monarchs easily fall into tyranny.
25. A monarch must be constrained by institutions.
26. Succession disputes destabilize monarchies.
27. Aristocracies must prevent concentration of wealth.
28. Aristocracies require rotation of office.
29. Democracy must exclude dependents who can be manipulated.
30. Large assemblies need strict procedural order.
31. Good institutions channel ambition into public service.
32. The military must be loyal to the state, not individuals.
33. Standing armies are dangerous if uncontrolled.
34. Citizen militias provide stability in democracies.
35. Public offices should have limited terms.
36. Corruption grows when offices are permanent.
37. Elections must prevent bribery and dominance by factions.
38. Public revenue must be transparent.
39. Taxation must reflect common benefit.
40. Harsh penalties weaken states by creating fear.
41. Mild punishments combined with certainty are most effective.
42. Laws should be few, clear, and easy to follow.
43. Good states foster arts and sciences.
44. Freedom of expression strengthens political order.
45. Religion must be subordinated to civil authority.
46. Civil peace requires tolerance of differing beliefs.
47. The sovereign must protect dissenters from persecution.
48. Liberty is preserved when no one has excessive power.
49. The strongest states empower rational cooperation.
50. The end of the state is freedom, not domination.

=== PRINCIPLES OF CARTESIAN PHILOSOPHY (1663) ===

1. Substance is that which exists in itself and is conceived through itself.
2. God is the only true self-subsistent substance.
3. Created substances depend on God for existence.
4. Mind and body are distinct substances.
5. Thought is the essence of mind.
6. Extension is the essence of body.
7. Corporeal substance is infinitely divisible.
8. Motion is the mode of extended substance.
9. Rest is also a mode of extension.
10. The laws of motion arise from God's immutability.
11. No vacuum exists in nature.
12. All motion is relative.
13. Bodies move only by external causes.
14. God conserves bodies in the same state unless acted on.
15. Collision follows determinate rules.
16. The quantity of motion in the universe is constant.
17. Ideas represent things either clearly or confusedly.
18. Clear and distinct ideas guarantee truth.
19. Error arises from judgment exceeding perception.
20. Will and intellect are distinct but inseparable.
21. Will is infinite; understanding is finite.
22. Freedom consists in acting from clear understanding.
23. The idea of God contains necessary existence.
24. Existence is included in the essence of God.
25. God is the cause of himself.
26. God is the cause of all things.
27. Created substances have no independent power.
28. There are no real qualities beyond extension.
29. Sensory qualities are confused ideas of motions.
30. Mind and body interact by divine institution.
31. Human error stems from inadequate ideas.
32. Matter is homogeneous in nature.
33. All physical change follows mechanical principles.
34. Soul is indivisible; body is divisible.
35. Animals are machines lacking thought.
36. Human sensation depends on the body's motions.
37. Understanding ideas requires intellectual intuition.
38. The mind knows itself more clearly than it knows the body.
39. Imagination is a mode of thought dependent on the body.
40. Memory is a bodily modification.
41. Eternity is the attribute of God alone.
42. Finite modes exist only through divine concurrence.
43. Extension cannot exist without God's sustaining action.
44. Mind cannot think without God's sustaining action.
45. The certainty of math derives from clear and distinct perception.
46. Physics must be derived from metaphysics.
47. True causation exists only in God.
48. Created causes are occasions for divine action.
49. The union of mind and body is known through experience, not reason.
50. Error is the privation of correct judgment.
