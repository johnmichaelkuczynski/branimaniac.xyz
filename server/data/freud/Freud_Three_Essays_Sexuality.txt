POSITION STATEMENT FOR FREUD
Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality (Drei Abhandlungen zur Sexualtheorie, 1905)

================================================================================
CORE THESIS
================================================================================

1. Sexuality is not limited to genital, reproductive activity but encompasses a broad range of pleasurable bodily experiences from infancy onward.

2. The sexual instinct is not unitary but is composed of component instincts that only later combine under genital primacy.

3. Infantile sexuality exists and is the foundation of adult sexual life.

4. Perversions are not degenerations but represent the persistence or reemergence of normal infantile sexual components.

================================================================================
ESSAY ONE: THE SEXUAL ABERRATIONS
================================================================================

ON THE SEXUAL OBJECT

5. The sexual instinct and the sexual object are merely soldered together - they are not intrinsically connected.

6. Homosexuality (inversion) is not a sign of degeneracy, innate depravity, or disease.

7. Exclusive homosexuality and exclusive heterosexuality are both endpoints of a continuum; bisexuality is the original human disposition.

8. The choice of sexual object is determined by experience, particularly infantile experience, not by innate biological factors alone.

9. Fetishism results from the overvaluation of a sexual object and fixation on an associated object or body part.

10. The fetish is a substitute for the mother's (fantasized) penis, whose absence the fetishist cannot accept.

ON THE SEXUAL AIM

11. Perversions represent lingering at preliminary sexual aims (looking, touching) that normally lead to genital union.

12. Sadism and masochism are the active and passive forms of the same component instinct.

13. Cruelty and sexuality are intimately connected; aggression is a component of the sexual instinct.

14. Scopophilia (pleasure in looking) and exhibitionism are paired component instincts.

15. The mucous membranes of all bodily orifices can serve as erotogenic zones.

16. Perversions become pathological only when they replace the normal sexual aim entirely or are pursued with fixation and exclusivity.

17. The disposition to perversion is universal - it is part of the normal human sexual constitution.

18. Neuroses are the negative of perversions - neurotics repress the same impulses that perverts act upon.

================================================================================
ESSAY TWO: INFANTILE SEXUALITY
================================================================================

THE PERIOD OF SEXUAL LATENCY

19. Infantile sexuality undergoes repression during the latency period (roughly ages 6-12), during which reaction formations (shame, disgust, morality) develop.

20. The latency period is not biologically fixed but is a product of civilization and education.

21. Sexual impulses during latency are sublimated into non-sexual aims (learning, social bonds).

THE MANIFESTATIONS OF INFANTILE SEXUALITY

22. Thumb-sucking demonstrates that pleasure can be obtained from the infant's own body independent of nourishment (autoerotic sexuality).

23. The first sexual object is the mother's breast; the infant's own body becomes a substitute.

24. Infantile sexuality is autoerotic - it seeks satisfaction from the subject's own body, not from an external object.

25. Erotogenic zones are regions of the body capable of producing sexual excitation when stimulated.

26. Any part of the body can become an erotogenic zone, but certain zones (oral, anal, genital) are predisposed.

THE ORAL PHASE

27. The oral zone is the first to produce sexual pleasure, attached initially to the vital function of feeding.

28. Sucking for pleasure (not nourishment) is the prototype of infantile sexual satisfaction.

29. The oral phase involves incorporation - the model of taking the object into oneself.

THE ANAL PHASE

30. The anal zone becomes a source of intense pleasure in the second year of life.

31. Children treat feces as a part of their own body, a gift, and a source of power over caregivers.

32. Retention and expulsion of feces provide pleasurable sensations and a sense of control.

33. Anal erotism contributes to later character traits: orderliness, parsimony, and obstinacy.

THE PHALLIC PHASE

34. The genital zone assumes primacy during the phallic phase (ages 3-5).

35. Infantile masturbation is universal and normal.

36. The penis (or clitoris) becomes the leading erotogenic zone for both sexes during this phase.

37. Children develop sexual theories to explain birth, sex differences, and parental intercourse.

38. The castration complex arises when the boy discovers that females lack a penis and fears the same fate.

39. Penis envy arises when the girl discovers her anatomical difference and feels deprived.

POLYMORPHOUS PERVERSITY

40. The child is polymorphously perverse - capable of any form of sexual pleasure, with no innate barriers.

41. The component instincts (oral, anal, scopophilic, sadistic) operate independently in childhood before integration.

42. Seduction can prematurely awaken specific component instincts and produce fixations.

THE SEXUAL RESEARCHES OF CHILDREN

43. Children are intensely curious about sexual matters, particularly the origin of babies and sex differences.

44. Children develop their own sexual theories independent of adult instruction.

45. The child's first theory is that everyone has a penis; discovery of sex difference creates the castration complex.

================================================================================
ESSAY THREE: THE TRANSFORMATIONS OF PUBERTY
================================================================================

46. Puberty brings the component instincts under the primacy of the genital zone.

47. The sexual aim becomes reproductive intercourse; foreplay represents the persistence of infantile component activities.

48. The finding of an object at puberty is a refinding - a return to the original (maternal) object.

49. The incest barrier redirects sexual aims away from family members toward outside objects.

50. The Oedipus complex - desire for the opposite-sex parent and rivalry with the same-sex parent - is universal.

51. Libido is the quantitative energy of the sexual instinct.

52. Libido can be attached to objects (object-libido) or directed toward the self (ego-libido/narcissism).

53. Fixation occurs when libido remains attached to earlier phases of development.

54. Regression is the return of libido to earlier fixation points under frustration.

DIFFERENTIATION OF MALE AND FEMALE

55. Both sexes are originally bisexual; differentiation occurs through development.

56. The girl must change both her leading erotogenic zone (clitoris to vagina) and her object (mother to father).

57. Feminine development is more complex than masculine development because of these additional tasks.

58. Activity is associated with masculinity, passivity with femininity, but these associations are not absolute.

================================================================================
THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

59. The sexual instinct has a source (erotogenic zone), an aim (satisfaction), and an object (what provides satisfaction).

60. Component instincts can undergo repression, sublimation, reaction formation, or reversal into their opposite.

61. Sublimation redirects sexual energy toward non-sexual, socially valued aims (art, science, work).

62. Neurotic symptoms are substitute satisfactions for repressed sexual impulses.

63. The choice of neurosis depends on which developmental phase produced the strongest fixations.

64. Constitution and experience interact - constitutional predisposition determines which experiences become traumatic.

================================================================================
IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

65. Sexual development is not linear but involves conflicts, regressions, and reorganizations.

66. Adult sexuality and character are shaped by infantile sexual experiences and their vicissitudes.

67. The understanding of normal sexuality requires understanding perversion and infantile sexuality.

68. Psychoanalytic treatment must address infantile sexual material to resolve neurotic symptoms.

69. Civilization requires the renunciation and sublimation of infantile sexual aims.

END OF POSITION STATEMENT
