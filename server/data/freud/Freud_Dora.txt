POSITION STATEMENTS: FREUD - FRAGMENT OF AN ANALYSIS OF A CASE OF HYSTERIA (DORA, 1905)

1. Hysterical symptoms are the expression of repressed sexual wishes and fantasies.
2. Dreams provide the royal road to understanding the unconscious conflicts underlying neurosis.
3. The analysis of dreams is indispensable for the complete elucidation of any case of hysteria.
4. Somatic compliance—a physical predisposition or weakness—is necessary for the localization of hysterical symptoms.
5. Hysterical symptoms can represent both the fulfillment of a wish and its punishment simultaneously.
6. Transference is the revival and displacement of earlier emotional attitudes onto the person of the analyst.
7. The failure to recognize and interpret transference can lead to the premature termination of analysis.
8. Disgust, when appearing inappropriately, is a symptom indicating repression of sexual excitation.
9. Hysterical aphonia can represent the symbolic expression of the absence of a loved object.
10. The reversal of affect is a characteristic mechanism in the formation of hysterical symptoms.
11. Hysterical identification allows a patient to express the experiences of multiple persons through symptoms.
12. Unconscious homosexual currents play a significant role in the psychical life of hysterics.
13. A hysterical symptom requires contributions from both an unconscious wish and a preconscious purpose.
14. Coughing and throat symptoms can symbolically represent oral sexual fantasies.
15. The bedwetting of childhood is frequently connected to masturbatory activity.
16. The displacement of affect from lower to upper parts of the body is characteristic of hysteria.
17. An unsatisfied woman whose sexual needs are not met may develop neurotic symptoms.
18. Fantasies of revenge and self-injury often underlie suicidal ideation in hysterical patients.
19. Resistance during analysis manifests through forgetting, blocking, and reluctance to associate.
20. The technique of pressure on the forehead can aid in recovering forgotten material.
21. Over-determination means that symptoms are caused by multiple converging psychical factors.
22. The repudiation of sexuality in consciousness leads to its symptomatic expression in the body.
23. A daughter may unconsciously identify with a mother she consciously despises.
24. Periodic illness can correspond to the duration of a fantasy pregnancy or similar unconscious process.
25. The analyst must not collude with the patient's resistances by accepting incomplete material.
